# Code of Conduct

This project adopts the [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/) v2.1 as its code of conduct. The key points are summarized below; the official full text is authoritative.

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes, and learning from the experience

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information without their explicit permission (such as the mapping between a real name and an anonymous ID, email addresses, or physical addresses)
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

**Project-specific note**: anonymous reviews are the core of this platform. Any attempt to de-anonymize other users (doxxing, or publicly inferring the author of a review) is a serious violation.

## Enforcement

Violations can be reported via [GitHub Issues](https://github.com/SihongShen/NYUSH-CSG/issues) (for privacy-sensitive matters, use the private channel in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md)). Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this code of conduct and may take actions against violators, including removing comments and temporary or permanent bans.
